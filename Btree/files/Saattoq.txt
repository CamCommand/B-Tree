   <p><b>Saattoq</b> (old spelling: <i>Sâtoq</i>) is a former settlement in the <a href="/wiki/Qaasuitsup" title="Qaasuitsup">Qaasuitsup</a> municipality in northwestern <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>. It was located in the north-central part of <a href="/wiki/Upernavik_Archipelago" title="Upernavik Archipelago">Upernavik Archipelago</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Tasiusaq_Bay" title="Tasiusaq Bay">Tasiusaq Bay</a>, straddling <a href="/wiki/Saattorsuaq_Island" title="Saattorsuaq Island">Saattorsuaq Island</a>, and the nearby <i>Saattoq</i> <a href="/wiki/Skerry" title="Skerry">skerry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-saga_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-saga-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saattoq&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Saattoq was populated in 1881. It was a very small village of fewer than 10 people at any time. It was abandoned in 1957, during the post-<a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">war</a> consolidation phase in northwestern Greenland,<sup id="cite_ref-hunt_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hunt-1">[1]</a></sup> with the survuving population moving to nearby <a href="/wiki/Nutaarmiut" title="Nutaarmiut">Nutaarmiut</a>. </p>
