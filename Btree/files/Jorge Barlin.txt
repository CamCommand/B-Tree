   <p><b>Jorge Barlin</b> (April 23, 1850, <a href="/wiki/Baao,_Camarines_Sur" title="Baao, Camarines Sur">Baao, Camarines Sur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> – September 4, 1909, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>) also known as <b>Jorge Barlin Imperial</b>, <b>Jorge Alfonso Imperial Barlin</b> and <b>Jorge Barlin y Imperial</b> following <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>, was the first <a href="/wiki/Filipino_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipino people">Filipino</a> consecrated a <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishop</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a>. He served as Archbishop of Caceres in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Caceres" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Caceres">Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Caceres</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> until 1909. He was the first Filipino and <a href="/wiki/Bicolano_people" title="Bicolano people">Bicolano</a> bishop and was parish priest and <a href="/wiki/Vicar_Forane" class="mw-redirect" title="Vicar Forane">Vicar Forane</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sorsogon" title="Sorsogon">Sorsogon</a> from 1887 to 1906.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jorge_Barlin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Barlin was born April 23, 1850 in <a href="/wiki/Baao,_Camarines_Sur" title="Baao, Camarines Sur">Baao, Camarines Sur</a>, the Philippines to Mateo Alfonso Barlin and Francisca Imperial and died in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> in 1909.<sup id="cite_ref-Findagrave_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Findagrave-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>He was ordained a priest on September 19, 1875 and consecrated a bishop on June 29, 1906 by Archbishop <a href="/wiki/Ambrose_Agius" title="Ambrose Agius">Ambrose Agius</a>, <a href="/wiki/O.S.B." class="mw-redirect" title="O.S.B.">O.S.B.</a> along with co-consecrators Archbishop <a href="/wiki/Jeremiah_James_Harty" title="Jeremiah James Harty">Jeremiah James Harty</a> and Bishop <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Zadok_Rooker" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick Zadok Rooker">Frederick Zadok Rooker</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Findagrave_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Findagrave-2">[2]</a></sup> "Barlin proved very capable and loyal, dealing a blow to the schismatic Iglesia Filipinia Independiente by resisting its recruitment efforts and winning a court battle over church property," according to <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Bishops_Conference_of_the_Philippines" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic Bishops Conference of the Philippines">Catholic Bishops Conference of the Philippines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PapalVisit2015_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PapalVisit2015-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>He died and was interred in that holy city.<sup id="cite_ref-Findagrave_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Findagrave-2">[2]</a></sup> Attempts to have his body returned to the Philippines were not successful.<sup id="cite_ref-Findagrave_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Findagrave-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>There is a monument commemorating Jorge Barlin called Plaza Barlin in <a href="/wiki/Naga,_Camarines_Sur" title="Naga, Camarines Sur">Naga, Camarines Sur</a> <sup id="cite_ref-naga.gov.ph_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-naga.gov.ph-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
