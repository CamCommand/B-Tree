   <p>The <b>Elkhart Express</b> were an <a href="/wiki/International_Basketball_League" title="International Basketball League">International Basketball League</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Elkhart,_Indiana" title="Elkhart, Indiana">Elkhart, Indiana</a>. The team won the 2006 and 2007 IBL championships. The Express officially released that they were folding on January 5, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On January 18, 2010, head coach and founder Daimon Beathea announced that the team would return for the <a href="/wiki/2010-11_ABA_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010-11 ABA season">2010 season</a>, but gym agreements had not been finalized.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_season">2006 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elkhart_Express&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 2006 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The Express played home games at North Side Gym in Elkhart, which was the home for the IBL Championship and All-Star game. The Express dominated the league for most of the season, and crowds of over 2,000 were routine. At home, the Express won the championship 119-108 over the <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Cyclones" class="mw-redirect" title="Columbus Cyclones">Columbus Cyclones</a>. </p>
