   <p><i><b>Strangers No More</b></i> is a 2010 short <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary film</a> about a school in <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, where children from 48 different countries and diverse backgrounds come together to learn. The parents of these children are among over 300,000 transnational migrant workers who have arrived in Israel—some with government authorization and others undocumented.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>The film follows three students as they struggle to acclimate to life in Israel and slowly unveil their stories of hardship. <i>Strangers No More</i> was shot on location at the Bialik-Rogozin School in Tel Aviv. It is produced and directed by <a href="/wiki/Karen_Goodman" title="Karen Goodman">Karen Goodman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Simon" title="Kirk Simon">Kirk Simon</a> of Simon &amp; Goodman Picture Company, whose films have received four <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nominations and three <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Emmy Awards">Emmy Awards</a>. It won best Short Documentary at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Awards</a> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premiere_and_screenings">Premiere and screenings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strangers_No_More&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premiere and screenings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p><i>Strangers No More</i> premiered on September 24, 2010, at the <a href="/wiki/Laemmle_Theatres" title="Laemmle Theatres">Laemmle Theatres</a> Sunset 5 Theatre in <a href="/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California" title="West Hollywood, California">West Hollywood, California</a>. It has also screened in cities worldwide through the Shorts International OSCAR®-Nominated Short Films 2011 program. On January 25, 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Motion_Picture_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences">Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences</a> announced <i>Strangers No More</i> as being nominated for the <a href="/wiki/83rd_Academy_Awards" title="83rd Academy Awards">83rd Academy Awards</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Best_Documentary_Short_Subject" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Documentary Short Subject">Best Documentary Short Subject</a> category and later won in the February 27, 2011 ceremony. </p>
   <p>The film made its television debut on <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> on December 5, 2011. </p>
