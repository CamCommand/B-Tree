  <div class="mw-parser-output">
   <p>The <b>Xinle culture</b> (新樂文化) (<a href="/wiki/5500_BC" class="mw-redirect" title="5500 BC">5500</a>–<a href="/wiki/4800_BC" class="mw-redirect" title="4800 BC">4800 BC</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> culture in northeast <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, found primarily around the lower <a href="/wiki/Liao_River" title="Liao River">Liao River</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Liaodong_Peninsula" title="Liaodong Peninsula">Liaodong Peninsula</a> in <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>. The culture showed evidence of <a href="/wiki/Millet" title="Millet">millet</a> cultivation and pig domestication. The <a href="/wiki/Type_site" title="Type site">type site</a> at Xinle was discovered in the <a href="/wiki/Huanggu_District" title="Huanggu District">Huanggu District</a> of <a href="/wiki/Shenyang" title="Shenyang">Shenyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>. </p>
   <p>The site is named after an old inn, in which grounds the remains were first discovered. </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinle_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The site of the ancient settlement was discovered in the grounds of an old accommodation block for an electrical factory. The accommodation block was called the Xinle Dormitory (新樂宿舍) and hence the discovery was named the Xinle Relic. When it was discovered that the settlement was that of a hitherto unknown civilization, the whole civilization was named after the relic and hence became known as the Xinle civilization. Although more recent discoveries in nearby areas have been extremely significant, especially one in <a href="/wiki/Xinmin,_Liaoning" title="Xinmin, Liaoning">Xinmin</a>, the original name has prevailed. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Excavations">Excavations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinle_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Excavations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>In 1973, excavations at the site discovered evidence for some 40 neolithic houses. Artifacts uncovered during the dig included stone tools, pottery, jade, bone tools, wood carvings and refined coal. </p>
   <p>In 1978, another dig uncovered yet more artifacts including one wooden carving that was some 7,200 years old, presumably a type of <a href="/wiki/Totem" title="Totem">totem</a> worshipped by the clan. No other find in the whole of <a href="/wiki/Shenyang" title="Shenyang">Shenyang</a> has been older, the find is also one of the oldest wooden carvings found anywhere in the world. </p>
   <p>The excavations also discovered two <a href="/wiki/Liao_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Liao Dynasty">Khitan</a> tombs from 1,000 years ago. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Museum">Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xinle_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>In 1984, the Museum of the Xinle Civilization (新樂遺址博物館) was founded. </p>
   <p>The museum is divided into two sections, north and south. The southern section contains displays of the various artifacts unearthed during the various excavations that have taken place on 44-acre (180,000&nbsp;m<sup>2</sup>) site. The northern section contains a reconstruction of a 7,000-year-old Xinle village. Some of the houses in the settlement contain representations of life 7,000 years ago. </p>
