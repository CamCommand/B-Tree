   <p><b>Petra Burka</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'p' in 'pie'">p</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span> <wbr><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/ɜːr/: 'ur' in 'fur'">ɜːr</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born November 17, 1946) is a <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> former competitive <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating" title="Figure skating">figure skater</a> and now <a href="/wiki/Coach_(sport)" title="Coach (sport)">coach</a>. She won the 1964 Olympic bronze medal in women's figure skating and the 1965 World championship in the sport. </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petra_Burka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Petra Burka was born in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, Netherlands on November 17, 1946,<sup id="cite_ref-IJSHF_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IJSHF-1">[1]</a></sup> the daughter of figure skating coach <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Burka" title="Ellen Burka">Ellen Burka</a> and a Czech-born artist, <a href="/wiki/Jan_Burka" title="Jan Burka">Jan Burka</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jt081125_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jt081125-2">[2]</a></sup> The family moved to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> in 1951.<sup id="cite_ref-IJSHF_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IJSHF-1">[1]</a></sup> Her parents divorced in the mid-1950s.<sup id="cite_ref-np131015_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-np131015-3">[3]</a></sup> Raised as Christians, Petra and her sister, Astra, were in their late teens when their mother told them about her background as a <a href="/wiki/Holocaust" class="mw-redirect" title="Holocaust">Holocaust</a> survivor.<sup id="cite_ref-jt081125_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jt081125-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Petra_Burka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <div class="thumb tright">
    <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
     <a href="/wiki/File:Petra_Burka_1965b.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Petra_Burka_1965b.jpg/220px-Petra_Burka_1965b.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="531" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Petra_Burka_1965b.jpg/330px-Petra_Burka_1965b.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Petra_Burka_1965b.jpg/440px-Petra_Burka_1965b.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1315" data-file-height="3173"></a> 
     <div class="thumbcaption">
      <div class="magnify">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Petra_Burka_1965b.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
      </div>Petra Burka in 1965
     </div>
    </div>
   </div> 
   <p>Petra Burka began skating lessons at the age of 10 or 11.<sup id="cite_ref-tsn090518_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsn090518-4">[4]</a></sup> Her mother, also her coach, was advised by <a href="/wiki/Osborne_Colson" title="Osborne Colson">Osborne Colson</a> of her talent.<sup id="cite_ref-tsn090518_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsn090518-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>At the 1962 Canadian Championships, Burka became the first woman to land a triple <a href="/wiki/Salchow_jump" title="Salchow jump">Salchow</a> and came away with the silver medal.<sup id="cite_ref-tsn090518_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsn090518-4">[4]</a></sup> She finished 4th in her <a href="/wiki/1962_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1962 World Figure Skating Championships">World Championship debut</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a> in the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-SCHOF_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCHOF-5">[5]</a></sup> The father of one of Ellen Burka's students, <a href="/wiki/Stafford_Smythe" title="Stafford Smythe">Stafford Smythe</a>, arranged for Petra – who was in need of extra ice time – to train at <a href="/wiki/Maple_Leaf_Gardens" title="Maple Leaf Gardens">Maple Leaf Gardens</a> at 7 a.m. every day before the hockey players arrived at 9:30.<sup id="cite_ref-tsn090518_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsn090518-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Burka won the first of her three consecutive senior national titles in 1964 and represented Canada at the <a href="/wiki/Figure_skating_at_the_1964_Winter_Olympics" title="Figure skating at the 1964 Winter Olympics">1964 Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Innsbruck" title="Innsbruck">Innsbruck</a>, winning the bronze medal.<sup id="cite_ref-r1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-r1-6">[6]</a></sup> Unaware that she was expected to skate an exhibition, she was brought back to the rink in a police car just before she was called onto the ice.<sup id="cite_ref-tsn090518_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsn090518-4">[4]</a></sup> Burka became the first Canadian skater to perform in the Soviet Union when she appeared in a two-week tour in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tsn090518_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsn090518-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Burka won bronze at the <a href="/wiki/1964_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1964 World Figure Skating Championships">1964 World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dortmund" title="Dortmund">Dortmund</a>. She was the gold medalist at the <a href="/wiki/1965_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1965 World Figure Skating Championships">1965 World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Springs,_Colorado" title="Colorado Springs, Colorado">Colorado Springs, Colorado</a>, becoming the first Canadian woman to win Worlds since <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Ann_Scott" title="Barbara Ann Scott">Barbara Ann Scott</a> in 1947. At the event, she also became the first woman to complete the triple Salchow at a World Championships.<sup id="cite_ref-SCHOF_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCHOF-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Her other achievements were winning Canada's Outstanding Athlete of the Year in 1964 and twice winning Canada's Outstanding Female Athlete of the Year in 1964 and 1965. In 1965 she was also inducted to the <a href="/wiki/Canada%27s_Sports_Hall_of_Fame" title="Canada's Sports Hall of Fame">Canada's Sports Hall of Fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-r1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-r1-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Burka took bronze at the <a href="/wiki/1966_World_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="1966 World Figure Skating Championships">1966 World Championships</a> and retired from competition. She signed with <a href="/wiki/Holiday_On_Ice" class="mw-redirect" title="Holiday On Ice">Holiday On Ice</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-SM196606_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SM196606-7">[7]</a></sup> skating with the tour until 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-CanEnc_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CanEnc-8">[8]</a></sup> Burka then took on <a href="/wiki/Coach_(sport)" title="Coach (sport)">coaching</a> as well as being a <a href="/wiki/Sportscaster" class="mw-redirect" title="Sportscaster">commentator</a> on figure skating events for <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Canadian Broadcasting Corporation">CBC</a> and <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> for Olympic, World, European and Canadian championships. </p>
   <p>Petra Burka was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Sports_Hall_of_Fame" title="Ontario Sports Hall of Fame">Ontario Sports Hall of Fame</a> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
