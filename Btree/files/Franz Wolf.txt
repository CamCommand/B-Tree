   <p><i><a href="/wiki/SS-Oberscharf%C3%BChrer" class="mw-redirect" title="SS-Oberscharführer">SS-Oberscharführer</a></i> <b>Franz Wolf</b> (born 9 April 1907) was a German Nazi senior squad leader serving with the <a href="/wiki/Action_T4" class="mw-redirect" title="Action T4">Action T4</a> <a href="/wiki/Involuntary_euthanasia" title="Involuntary euthanasia">forced euthanasia</a> program, and later, at the <a href="/wiki/Sobib%C3%B3r_extermination_camp" class="mw-redirect" title="Sobibór extermination camp">Sobibór extermination camp</a> in <a href="/wiki/Occupied_Poland" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupied Poland">occupied Poland</a> during the most deadly phase of <a href="/wiki/The_Holocaust" title="The Holocaust">the Holocaust</a>, codenamed <a href="/wiki/Operation_Reinhard" title="Operation Reinhard">Operation Reinhard</a>. Leading a normal life in West Germany for the next twenty years,<sup id="cite_ref-Blatt_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blatt-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-H.E.A.R.T_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H.E.A.R.T-2">[2]</a></sup> along with thousands of war criminals protected by <a href="/wiki/Konrad_Adenauer" title="Konrad Adenauer">Konrad Adenauer</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Simon_Wiesenthal_Center_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Simon_Wiesenthal_Center-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hartmann_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hartmann-4">[4]</a></sup> Wolf was arrested in 1964,<sup id="cite_ref-Blatt_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blatt-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-H.E.A.R.T_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H.E.A.R.T-2">[2]</a></sup> and indicted during the <a href="/wiki/Sobib%C3%B3r_trial" class="mw-redirect" title="Sobibór trial">Sobibór trial</a> with participating in the murder of 115,000 Jews. On 20 December 1966,<sup id="cite_ref-H.E.A.R.T_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H.E.A.R.T-2">[2]</a></sup> the court in Hagen sentenced him to eight years in prison for taking part in the mass murder of "at least 39,000 Jews".<sup id="cite_ref-Blatt_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blatt-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-H.E.A.R.T_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H.E.A.R.T-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franz_Wolf_(SS_officer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Little is known about his private life except that Wolf came from <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%BD_Krumlov" title="Český Krumlov">Krummau</a>. He was in the Czechoslovak Army and in the German <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">Wehrmacht</a>, before he was posted to <a href="/wiki/Hadamar_Euthanasia_Centre" title="Hadamar Euthanasia Centre">Hadamar Euthanasia Centre</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg" title="Heidelberg">Heidelberg</a> Psychiatric Clinic where the killing of patients deemed beyond the reach of therapy was taking place.<sup id="cite_ref-Seeman_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seeman-5">[5]</a></sup> In the name of science, Wolf photographed the mentally ill before they were gassed.<sup id="cite_ref-Kommando_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommando-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Together with his brother Josef Wolf (1900–1943), he was sent to Sobibór <a href="/wiki/Extermination_camp" title="Extermination camp">extermination camp</a> in German <a href="/wiki/Occupied_Poland" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupied Poland">occupied Poland</a> sometime in early March 1943, as a specialist in euthanasia. The mass gassing operations at Sobibór were at full throttle already since mid-May 1942. In total, up to 200,000 (or more)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> mostly Jewish prisoners were murdered there. Wolf was in Sobibór until the prisoner uprising. He liked to hang around the victims' "barber shop" where his brother was a squad leader<sup id="cite_ref-Kommando_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kommando-6">[6]</a></sup> and watch naked women having their hair shorn off by the <i>Sonderkommando</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Interviews_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Interviews-8">[8]</a></sup> He supervised the sorting barracks where belongings of the victims were processed, but also led the <i>Waldkommando</i> forest brigade,<sup id="cite_ref-Interviews_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Interviews-8">[8]</a></sup> cutting trees for the fuelling of corpse cremation <a href="/wiki/Pyre" title="Pyre">pyres</a> in the camp's killing zone.<sup id="cite_ref-Waldkommando_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Waldkommando-9">[9]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arrest">Arrest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franz_Wolf_(SS_officer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Arrest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Wolf was presented with an arrest warrant in 1964 at Eppelheim<sup id="cite_ref-ZEIT_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZEIT-10">[10]</a></sup> as one of a selected twelve former members of the SS camp personnel, which constituted about a quarter of the German staff there. The rest went on to live normal lives. In 1966 the court in Hagen sentenced Wolf to eight years in prison for his role as an overseer of a <a href="/wiki/Forced_labour_under_German_rule_during_World_War_II" title="Forced labour under German rule during World War II">slave labour</a> commando that sorted the belongings of victims already "processed". Wolf, in his work as the warehouse clerk, was charged with personally killing one Jew and with helping to murder an additional 115,000 Jews. He was found guilty of having assisted in the murder of "at least 39,000 Jews", a number chosen arbitrarily for judicial purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-Urteil_LG_Hagen_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Urteil_LG_Hagen-11">[11]</a></sup> Sentenced to eight years in prison, Wolf, age 58 (at the time of his arrest), likely served at least a part of the sentence. This is all that is known about him,<sup id="cite_ref-Blatt_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blatt-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-H.E.A.R.T_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H.E.A.R.T-2">[2]</a></sup> other than he lived somewhere in Bavaria. His brother, <i><a href="/wiki/SS-Scharf%C3%BChrer" class="mw-redirect" title="SS-Scharführer">SS-Scharführer</a></i> Josef Wolf, was killed in the Sobibór uprising.<sup id="cite_ref-Wolf_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wolf-12">[12]</a></sup> </p>
