   <p>In <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, the <b>Noether inequality</b>, named after <a href="/wiki/Max_Noether" title="Max Noether">Max Noether</a>, is a property of <a href="/wiki/Compact_space" title="Compact space">compact</a> minimal <a href="/wiki/Complex_surface" class="mw-redirect" title="Complex surface">complex surfaces</a> that restricts the topological type of the underlying topological <a href="/wiki/4-manifold" title="4-manifold">4-manifold</a>. It holds more generally for minimal projective surfaces of general type over an algebraically closed field. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formulation_of_the_inequality">Formulation of the inequality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noether_inequality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formulation of the inequality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

   <p>For complex surfaces, an alternative formulation expresses this inequality in terms of topological invariants of the underlying real oriented four manifold. Since a surface of general type is a <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A4hler_manifold" title="Kähler manifold">Kähler</a> surface, the dimension of the maximal positive subspace in intersection form on the second cohomology is given by <i>b</i><sub>+</sub> =&nbsp;1&nbsp;+&nbsp;2<i>p</i><sub>g</sub>. Moreover, by the <a href="/wiki/Hirzebruch_signature_theorem" title="Hirzebruch signature theorem">Hirzebruch signature theorem</a> <i>c</i><sub>1</sub><sup>2</sup> (<i>X</i>)&nbsp;=&nbsp;2<i>e</i>&nbsp;+&nbsp;3<i>σ</i>, where <i>e</i> = <i>c</i><sub>2</sub>(<i>X</i>) is the topological <a href="/wiki/Euler_characteristic" title="Euler characteristic">Euler characteristic</a> and <i>σ</i> = <i>b</i><sub>+</sub>&nbsp;−&nbsp;<i>b</i><sub>−</sub> is the signature of the <a href="/wiki/Intersection_form_(4-manifold)" title="Intersection form (4-manifold)">intersection form</a>.
