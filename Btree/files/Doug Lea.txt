   <p><b>Douglas S. Lea</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> and current head of the <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> department at <a href="/wiki/SUNY_Oswego" class="mw-redirect" title="SUNY Oswego">State University of New York at Oswego</a>, where he specializes in <a href="/wiki/Concurrent_programming" class="mw-redirect" title="Concurrent programming">concurrent programming</a> and the design of <a href="/wiki/Concurrent_data_structure" title="Concurrent data structure">concurrent data structures</a>. He was on the Executive Committee of the <a href="/wiki/Java_Community_Process" title="Java Community Process">Java Community Process</a> and chaired JSR 166, which added <a href="/wiki/Concurrency_(computer_science)" title="Concurrency (computer science)">concurrency</a> <a href="/wiki/Utility_(computer_science)" class="mw-redirect" title="Utility (computer science)">utilities</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java programming language</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Java_concurrency" title="Java concurrency">Java concurrency</a>). On October 22, 2010, Doug Lea notified the <a href="/wiki/Java_Community_Process" title="Java Community Process">Java Community Process</a> Executive Committee he would not stand for reelection.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Lea was re-elected as an at-large member for the 2012 <a href="/wiki/OpenJDK" title="OpenJDK">OpenJDK</a> governing board.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>He wrote <i>Concurrent Programming in Java: Design Principles and Patterns</i>, one of the first books about the subject. It is currently in its second edition. He is also the author of <a href="/wiki/Malloc#dlmalloc" class="mw-redirect" title="Malloc">dlmalloc</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> a widely used public-domain implementation of <a href="/wiki/Malloc" class="mw-redirect" title="Malloc">malloc</a>. </p>
   <p>In 2013, he became a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Fellows_of_the_Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="List of Fellows of the Association for Computing Machinery">Fellow of the Association for Computing Machinery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
