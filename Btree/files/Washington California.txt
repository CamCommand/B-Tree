   <p><b>Washington</b> (originally, <b>Indiana Camp</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-CGN_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CGN-4">[4]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Nevada_County,_California" title="Nevada County, California">Nevada County, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gnis_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gnis-3">[3]</a></sup> Washington is located on the banks of the South Fork of The <a href="/wiki/Yuba_River" title="Yuba River">Yuba River</a> and has a population of approximately two hundred people. There is a <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">hotel</a>/<a href="/wiki/Bar_(establishment)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bar (establishment)">bar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">restaurant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grocery" class="mw-redirect" title="Grocery">grocery</a> store, a one-room schoolhouse that has educated students continuously for one hundred years, and two trailer park campgrounds. The elevation is 3,652 feet (1,113&nbsp;m). The population was 185 at the 2010 census. </p>
   <p>The population fluctuates seasonally and the town businesses rely on the tourist trade as the population increases in the summer. It was settled during the <a href="/wiki/California_Gold_Rush" title="California Gold Rush">California Gold Rush</a> in 1849 and produced a large amount of placer gold. Hard rock mines were established soon afterword and were very productive. Washington is the only settlement in the vicinity to have survived to this day. There remains today much evidence of placer <a href="/wiki/Gold_mining" title="Gold mining">gold mining</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_mining" title="Hydraulic mining">hydraulic mining</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Underground_mining_(hard_rock)" title="Underground mining (hard rock)">hard rock mining</a>. During the mining period there were a large number of <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Chinese" title="Overseas Chinese">Chinese</a> living there. </p>
   <p>Washington's biggest businesses are its two campsites, Gene's Pine Aire Campground and the River Rest, which has been in business since the 1960s through different owners. toghost.com Sara Ross-Samko] is making a feature length <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary</a> portrait of the town. </p>
   <p>The ZIP Code is 95986. The community is inside <a href="/wiki/Area_code_530" title="Area code 530">area code 530</a>. </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Washington,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The place was founded in 1849 by miners from <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, and was originally named Indiana Camp.<sup id="cite_ref-CGN_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CGN-4">[4]</a></sup> It was renamed Washington in 1850.<sup id="cite_ref-CGN_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CGN-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>The Washington South Yuba post office operated here from 1852 to 1854.<sup id="cite_ref-CGN_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CGN-4">[4]</a></sup> The Washington post office opened in 1862.<sup id="cite_ref-CGN_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CGN-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Washington,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the CDP covers an area of 1.9 square miles (4.9&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup>), all of it land. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Washington,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> reported that Washington had a population of 185. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 97.4 people per square mile (37.6/km²). The racial makeup of Washington was 166 (89.7%) <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 1 (0.5%) <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 4 (2.2%) <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0 (0.0%) <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0 (0.0%) <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 4 (2.2%) from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 10 (5.4%) from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 11 persons (5.9%). </p>
   <p>The Census reported that 169 people (91.4% of the population) lived in households, 16 (8.6%) lived in non-institutionalized group quarters, and 0 (0%) were institutionalized. </p>
   <p>There were 90 households, out of which 11 (12.2%) had children under the age of 18 living in them, 21 (23.3%) were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">opposite-sex married couples</a> living together, 5 (5.6%) had a female householder with no husband present, 9 (10.0%) had a male householder with no wife present. There were 12 (13.3%) <a href="/wiki/POSSLQ" title="POSSLQ">unmarried opposite-sex partnerships</a>, and 1 (1.1%) <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_partnerships" class="mw-redirect" title="Same-sex partnerships">same-sex married couples or partnerships</a>. 43 households (47.8%) were made up of individuals and 10 (11.1%) had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 1.88. There were 35 <a href="/wiki/Family_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Family (U.S. Census)">families</a> (38.9% of all households); the average family size was 2.43. </p>
   <p>The population age was spread out with 25 people (13.5%) under the age of 18, 12 people (6.5%) aged 18 to 24, 31 people (16.8%) aged 25 to 44, 86 people (46.5%) aged 45 to 64, and 31 people (16.8%) who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 51.7 years. For every 100 females, there were 160.6 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 138.8 males. </p>
   <p>There were 131 housing units at an average density of 69.0 per square mile (26.6/km²), of which 55 (61.1%) were owner-occupied, and 35 (38.9%) were occupied by renters. The homeowner vacancy rate was 0%; the rental vacancy rate was 2.8%. 110 people (59.5% of the population) lived in owner-occupied housing units and 59 people (31.9%) lived in rental housing units. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Washington,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>In the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Legislature" title="California State Legislature">state legislature</a>, Washington is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_1st_State_Senate_district" title="California's 1st State Senate district">the 1st Senate District</a>, seat currently <a href="/wiki/Casual_vacancy" title="Casual vacancy">vacant</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/California%27s_1st_State_Assembly_district" title="California's 1st State Assembly district">the 1st Assembly District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Republican_Party" title="California Republican Party">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Brian_Dahle" title="Brian Dahle">Brian Dahle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Federally, Washington is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_1st_congressional_district" title="California's 1st congressional district">California's 1st congressional district</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Doug_LaMalfa" title="Doug LaMalfa">Doug LaMalfa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
