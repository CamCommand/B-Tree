   <p><b>Los Teques</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation:&nbsp;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[los ˈtekes]</a></span>) is the capital city of the <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuelan</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Miranda_(state)" title="Miranda (state)">Miranda</a> and the center of the <a href="/wiki/Altos_Mirandinos_Metropolitan_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Altos Mirandinos Metropolitan Area">Altos Mirandinos Metropolitan Area</a>. Its population is 140,617 (2001). </p>

   <p>From 1772 onwards, the Spanish inhabitants of <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_de_Los_Altos" class="mw-redirect" title="San Pedro de Los Altos">San Pedro de Los Altos</a> began to relocate to a village that had only 99 inhabitants. Five years later, they founded their new settlement as Los Teques on 21 October 1777. The new town's name was derived from the Aractoeques Carabs, an indigenous tribe that once inhabited the area. On February 13, 1927, the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Miranda_(state)" title="Miranda (state)">Miranda State</a> was moved to this city from <a href="/wiki/Petare" title="Petare">Petare</a> (before being in Petare, the capital of Miranda was in <a href="/wiki/Ocumare_del_Tuy" title="Ocumare del Tuy">Ocumare del Tuy</a>). </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Los_Teques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Temperature: Varies from 18 and 26 degrees Celsius (64.4 and 78.8 degrees Fahrenheit). </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Los_Teques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>On November 3, 2006, President <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ch%C3%A1vez" title="Hugo Chávez">Hugo Chávez</a> inaugurated the <a href="/wiki/Los_Teques_Metro" title="Los Teques Metro">Los Teques Metro</a>. This metro system is connected to the <a href="/wiki/Caracas_Metro" title="Caracas Metro">Caracas Metro</a>. </p>
