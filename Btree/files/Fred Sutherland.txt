   <p><a href="/wiki/Lieutenant-general_(Canada)" title="Lieutenant-general (Canada)">Lieutenant-General</a> <b>Fred Sutherland</b> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Military_Merit_(Canada)" title="Order of Military Merit (Canada)">CMM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces_Decoration" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian Forces Decoration">CD</a> (born February 1942) is a <a href="/wiki/Canadian" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian">Canadian</a> retired air force general who was <a href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Royal_Canadian_Air_Force" title="Commander of the Royal Canadian Air Force">Commander, Air Command</a> from 1989 to 1991 and <a href="/wiki/Vice_Chief_of_the_Defence_Staff_(Canada)" title="Vice Chief of the Defence Staff (Canada)">Vice Chief of the Defence Staff</a> from 1991 to 1992. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fred_Sutherland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Sutherland joined the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian Air Force">Canadian Air Force</a> in 1960<sup id="cite_ref-contact_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contact-1">[1]</a></sup> and, after graduating from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Military_College_of_Canada" title="Royal Military College of Canada">Royal Military College of Canada</a>, trained as a fighter pilot.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-2">[2]</a></sup> He became Commanding Officer of <a href="/wiki/439_Combat_Support_Squadron" title="439 Combat Support Squadron">439 Tactical Fighter Squadron</a> at <a href="/wiki/CFB_Baden-Soellingen" class="mw-redirect" title="CFB Baden-Soellingen">CFB Baden-Soellingen</a> in 1976, Executive Assistant to the <a href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Royal_Canadian_Air_Force" title="Commander of the Royal Canadian Air Force">Commander, Air Command</a> in 1978 and Director of Air Operations, Training and Nuclear Weapons at the <a href="/wiki/National_Defence_Headquarters_(Canada)" title="National Defence Headquarters (Canada)">National Defence Headquarters</a> in 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-2">[2]</a></sup> He went on to be Commander of <a href="/wiki/CFB_Cold_Lake" title="CFB Cold Lake">CFB Cold Lake</a> in July 1982, was seconded to the <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_Office_(Canada)" title="Privy Council Office (Canada)">Privy Council Office</a> in July 1984 and became Chief of Personnel Development at the National Defence Headquarters in July 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-2">[2]</a></sup> His last appointments were as Commander, Air Command in August 1989 and <a href="/wiki/Vice_Chief_of_the_Defence_Staff_(Canada)" title="Vice Chief of the Defence Staff (Canada)">Vice Chief of the Defence Staff</a> in August 1991 before retiring in June 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>In retirement he became Deputy Minister of Industry, Trade and Tourism for the <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Province of Manitoba</a> and then Corporate Vice-President Human Resources with <a href="/wiki/McCain_Foods" title="McCain Foods">McCain Foods</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-contact_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contact-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
