   <p><b>Autruche</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ardennes_(department)" title="Ardennes (department)">Ardennes</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Est" title="Grand Est">Grand Est</a> region of northern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. </p>
   <p>The inhabitants of the commune are known as <i>Autruchiens</i> or <i>Autruchiennes</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autruche&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Autruche is located some 43&nbsp;km east by south-east of <a href="/wiki/Rethel" title="Rethel">Rethel</a> and 20&nbsp;km north-east of <a href="/wiki/Vouziers" title="Vouziers">Vouziers</a>. Access to the commune is by the D947 from <a href="/wiki/Germont" title="Germont">Germont</a> in the south-west which passes through the southern corner of the commune and continues east to <a href="/wiki/Bar-l%C3%A8s-Buzancy" title="Bar-lès-Buzancy">Bar-lès-Buzancy</a>. The D12 also comes from <a href="/wiki/Authe" title="Authe">Authe</a> in the north-west and joins the D947 in the commune. Access to the village is by a local road branching from the D947 which goes north to the village. The commune is entirely farmland except for some lakes in the south.<sup id="cite_ref-Google_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Google-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>The <a href="/wiki/Bar_(river)" title="Bar (river)">Bar</a> river forms the southern border of the commune as it flows west then follows the <a href="/wiki/Canal_des_Ardennes" title="Canal des Ardennes">Canal des Ardennes</a> to eventually join the <a href="/wiki/Meuse_(river)" class="mw-redirect" title="Meuse (river)">Meuse</a> at <a href="/wiki/Vrigne-Meuse" title="Vrigne-Meuse">Vrigne-Meuse</a>. The <i>Ruisseau de Saint Pierremont</i> flows from the north of the commune towards the south-west and continues to join the Bar west of the commune. The <i>Ruisseau du Clageot</i> rises in the north of the commune and flows south-west down the centre of the commune and also joins the Bar on the southern border.<sup id="cite_ref-Google_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Google-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
