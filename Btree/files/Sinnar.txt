   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinnar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>At its peak, the Seuna or <a href="/wiki/Yadava_dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Yadava dynasty">Yadava dynasty</a> (850 - 1334) ruled a kingdom stretching from the <a href="/wiki/Tungabhadra" class="mw-redirect" title="Tungabhadra">Tungabhadra</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Narmada" class="mw-redirect" title="Narmada">Narmada</a> Rivers, including present-day Maharashtra, north <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a> and parts of <a href="/wiki/Madhya_Pradesh" title="Madhya Pradesh">Madhya Pradesh</a>. The capital was at Devagiri, now known as <a href="/wiki/Daulatabad,_Maharashtra" title="Daulatabad, Maharashtra">Daulatabad</a> in Maharashtra. The reign of the Yadava dynasty declined after the conquest of the Daulatabad Fort by <a href="/wiki/Alauddin_Khalji" title="Alauddin Khalji">Alauddin Khalji</a> in 1294. Further conquest by Malik Kafur, Alauddin's general, in 1312 resulted in the killing of the members of the Yadava clan ending this illustrious dynasty. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinnar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Sinnar is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Sinnar&amp;params=19.85_N_74.0_E_"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">19°51′N</span> <span class="longitude">74°00′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">19.85°N 74.0°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">19.85; 74.0</span></span></span></a></span>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It has an average elevation of 651.4&nbsp;metres (2135&nbsp;feet). Sinnar is one of the major industrial zones of Malegaon (<a href="/wiki/MIDC" class="mw-redirect" title="MIDC">MIDC</a>) built around the city of <a href="/wiki/Nashik" title="Nashik">Nashik</a> which have multiple international production companies. It lies 30&nbsp;km southeast of <a href="/wiki/Nashik" title="Nashik">Nashik</a> city on the Pune - Nashik Highway. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinnar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>As of 2001<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sinnar&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> India <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Sinnar had a population of 65,299. Males constitute 52% of the population and females 48%. Sinnar has an average literacy rate of 71%, higher than the national average of 59.5%: male literacy is 77%, and female literacy is 64%. In Sinnar, 15% of the population is under 6 years of age. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinnar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The <a href="/wiki/Gondeshwar_Temple,_Sinnar" title="Gondeshwar Temple, Sinnar">Gondeshwar Temple</a>, a 11th-12th century temple dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Shiva" title="Shiva">Shiva</a>, is located in Sinnar. </p>
   <p>The <a href="/wiki/Gargoti_Museum" title="Gargoti Museum">Gargoti Museum</a> houses a collection of mineral specimens native to the region. </p>
