   <p><b>Balally</b> (<a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i lang="ga">Baile Amhlaoibh</i>, meaning "Olafs' Town"), <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, is a residential area at the southern end of <a href="/wiki/Dundrum,_Dublin" title="Dundrum, Dublin">Dundrum, Dublin</a> between Dundrum <a href="/wiki/Village" title="Village">village</a> and the Sandyford Industrial Estate in <a href="/wiki/Sandyford" title="Sandyford">Sandyford</a>. </p>
   <p>Balally Parish itself reaches from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ardglas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ardglas (page does not exist)">Ardglas</a> to the <a href="/wiki/M50_motorway_(Ireland)" title="M50 motorway (Ireland)">M50</a> motorway. It has two primary schools (St. Olaf's National School] and Queen of Angels) and two secondary schools (St. Tiernan's and <a href="/wiki/Wesley_College,_Dublin" class="mw-redirect" title="Wesley College, Dublin">Wesley College</a>). Balally is part of the <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a> of Greater Dublin, in the county of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAn_Laoghaire%E2%80%93Rathdown" title="Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown">Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown</a>. The area contains a <a href="/wiki/Luas" title="Luas">Luas</a> tram station, located near the southern entrance to the new <a href="/wiki/Dundrum_Town_Centre" title="Dundrum Town Centre">Dundrum Town Centre</a>. </p>
   <p>It was originally farmland and is located at the foot of the <a href="/wiki/Wicklow_Mountains" title="Wicklow Mountains">Wicklow Mountains</a>. It consists of: Balally Drive, Balally Hill, Balally Grove, Balally Close, Balally Park, Balally Avenue &amp; Balally Road, and Sandyford Road. It has been re-connected to Sandyford Road at Balally Hill after access was closed off due to cars cutting through the estate to beat the traffic congestion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balally&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The area is named after Olaf (<i>Amhlaoibh</i>), the first King of Viking Dublin, who is reputed to have built a fort in the locality. The place name <i>Baile Mhic Amhlaoibh</i>, meaning "the town of the son of Olaf" was anglicised as <i>Balally</i>. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balally&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The local primary school, St. Olaf's National School,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> has more than 350 students. The Church of the Ascension of the Lord, the local <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> church, was built in 1982. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Churches">Churches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balally&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Churches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The Church of the Ascension of the Lord, the local <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> church, was built in October 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balally&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>It is serviced by the buses: 44, 44B and 116. The 44 travels on to Enniskerry in the south and Larkhill to the north and runs hourly seven days a week. The 44B service operates Monday to Friday, in the early morning and early evening between Dundrum and Glencullen. The 116 also serves the area, going from Lesson Street once a day Monday to Friday in the afternoon and once in the morning to Parnell Square. </p>

   <p>The light rail rapid transit system, <a href="/wiki/Luas" title="Luas">Luas</a> stops in Ballaly and connects the suburb to Bride's Glen in the south and the City Centre and <a href="/wiki/Broombridge_railway_station" title="Broombridge railway station">Broombridge</a> in the North. </p>
   <p>Some proposals for the Dublin Metro, <a href="/wiki/MetroLink_(Dublin)" title="MetroLink (Dublin)">Metrolink</a>, proposed a stop alongside Ballaly's Luas station on its way from Swords to Sandyford.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> However, as of February 2019, it was questioned whether this section of the line would be completed as originally proposed.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
