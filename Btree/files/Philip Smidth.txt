   <p><b>Philip Smidth</b> (3 May 1855 – 21 June 1938) was a prolific Danish architect in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. His works included, commercial properties, high-end apartment buildings, hotels and hospitals. He worked in the <a href="/wiki/Historicism_(art)" title="Historicism (art)">Historicist</a> style. Two of his works, <a href="/wiki/Liselund_Ny_Slot" title="Liselund Ny Slot">Liselund Ny Slot</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B8n" title="Møn">Møn</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gefion_and_Gylfe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gefion and Gylfe (page does not exist)">Gefion and Gylfe</a> in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>, have been listed by the <a href="/wiki/Danish_Heritage_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Danish Heritage Agency">Danish Heritage Agency</a>. </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Philip_Smidth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Philip Smidth was born in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8nnede" title="Rønnede">Rønnede</a> to the south of <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>. His parents were, <i>kammerråd</i> and later <i>justitsråd</i> Jens Frederik Julius Beck Smidth and Anna Henriette née Høyer. After passing his preliminary exams, he apprentished as a timber and then studied at the Yechnical Society's School and attending C. V. Nielsens Regneskole. He enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Danish_Academy_of_Fine_Arts" title="Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts">Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts</a> in January 1873 and graduated in March 1882.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>
