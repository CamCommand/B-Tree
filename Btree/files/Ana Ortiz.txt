   <p><b>Ana Ortiz</b> (born January 25, 1971)<sup id="cite_ref-tvg_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> actress and singer. Having pursued a career in ballet and singing from a young age, she eventually attended <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_(Philadelphia)" title="University of the Arts (Philadelphia)">University of the Arts</a>. Ortiz began her acting career in theatre, in early 2000s starred in the short-lived <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> sitcoms <i><a href="/wiki/Kristin_(TV_series)" title="Kristin (TV series)">Kristin</a></i> (2001) and <i><a href="/wiki/A.U.S.A." title="A.U.S.A.">A.U.S.A.</a></i> (2003), and had recurring roles on <i><a href="/wiki/Over_There_(American_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Over There (American TV series)">Over There</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Legal" title="Boston Legal">Boston Legal</a></i>. </p>
   <p>Ortiz garnered widespread attention for her role as <a href="/wiki/Hilda_Suarez" title="Hilda Suarez">Hilda Suarez</a> in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy-drama" title="Comedy-drama">comedy-drama</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i> from 2006 to 2010. She also appeared in such films like <i><a href="/wiki/Labor_Pains" title="Labor Pains">Labor Pains</a></i> (2009) and <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Mommas:_Like_Father,_Like_Son" title="Big Mommas: Like Father, Like Son">Big Mommas: Like Father, Like Son</a></i> (2011), and starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Girl_Lost:_The_Delimar_Vera_Story" title="Little Girl Lost: The Delimar Vera Story">Little Girl Lost: The Delimar Vera Story</a></i> (2008). From 2013 to 2016, Ortiz starred as Marisol Suarez in the <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> television comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Devious_Maids" title="Devious Maids">Devious Maids</a></i>, for which she received the <a href="/wiki/Imagen_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Imagen Award">Imagen Award for Best Actress - Television</a>. </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career_in_theatre">Early life and career in theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and career in theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
   <p>Ortiz was born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-tvg_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> and is the daughter of Angel Ortiz, a former <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_City_Council" title="Philadelphia City Council">Philadelphia City Council</a> member of Puerto Rican descent, and an <a href="/wiki/Irish-American" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish-American">Irish-American</a> mother,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Kathleen Kulhman.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As a child, Ortiz studied ballet for eight years, until the pain of dancing <a href="/wiki/En_pointe" class="mw-redirect" title="En pointe">en pointe</a> forced her to pursue a different artistic discipline. Ortiz graduated from <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_(Philadelphia)" title="University of the Arts (Philadelphia)">University of the Arts</a> in Philadelphia.<sup id="cite_ref-tvg_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

   <p>Switching to singing, Ortiz attended the <a href="/wiki/Fiorello_H._LaGuardia_High_School_of_Music_%26_Art_and_Performing_Arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music &amp; Art and Performing Arts">Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music &amp; Art and Performing Arts</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and later the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_(Philadelphia)" title="University of the Arts (Philadelphia)">University of the Arts</a> in Philadelphia before making her professional stage debut in a regional theater production of <i>Dangerous Liaisons</i>. Her additional stage credits include portraying Chrissy in a European touring production of <i><a href="/wiki/Hair_(musical)" title="Hair (musical)">Hair</a></i>, regional theater productions such as <i>Dog Lady and the Cuban Swimmer</i> and in the <a href="/wiki/South_Coast_Repertory" title="South Coast Repertory">South Coast Repertory</a> Theater's production of <i>References to Salvador Dalí Make Me Hot</i>. After joining LAByrinth Theatre Company,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> she appeared <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> in <i>In Arabia, We'd All Be Kings</i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a>-winning actor <a href="/wiki/Philip_Seymour_Hoffman" title="Philip Seymour Hoffman">Philip Seymour Hoffman</a> and named one of the 10-best plays of 1999 by the magazine <i>Time Out New York</i>. </p>
   <p>Ortiz appeared in several television shows including <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_Loves_Raymond" title="Everybody Loves Raymond">Everybody Loves Raymond</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Commander_In_Chief" class="mw-redirect" title="Commander In Chief">Commander In Chief</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Adventures_of_Old_Christine" title="The New Adventures of Old Christine">The New Adventures of Old Christine</a></i>. She had her first series regular role in short-lived NBC comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Kristin_(TV_series)" title="Kristin (TV series)">Kristin</a></i> (2001). She later starred in another short-lived sitcom, called <i><a href="/wiki/A.U.S.A." title="A.U.S.A.">A.U.S.A.</a></i> in 2003, and later had recurring roles in the FX drama <i><a href="/wiki/Over_There_(American_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Over There (American TV series)">Over There</a></i>, and ABC series <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Legal" title="Boston Legal">Boston Legal</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-tvg_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006-2010:Ugly_Betty_and_other_roles">2006-2010:<i>Ugly Betty</i> and other roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006-2010:Ugly Betty and other roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
   <p>In 2006, Ortiz was cast as <a href="/wiki/Hilda_Suarez" title="Hilda Suarez">Hilda Suarez</a>, the lead character's sister, in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> comedy-drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i>. Originally, Ortiz had auditioned for the <a href="/wiki/Betty_Suarez" title="Betty Suarez">Betty Suarez</a> role, saying in 2008, "I was super plain. I just put on some glasses and kept my hair kind of flat and layered on the clothing", describing what she wore when auditioning for the role of Betty. "I just wanted them to remember me, so that I could be involved in the future".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>

   <p>In addition to her role on <i>Ugly Betty</i>, Ortiz was active in films, which include starring roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Tortilla_Heaven" title="Tortilla Heaven">Tortilla Heaven</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Labor_Pains" title="Labor Pains">Labor Pains</a></i>. She also had a voice role in <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Gotham_Knight" title="Batman: Gotham Knight">Batman: Gotham Knight</a></i>, and starred in the made-for-television movie <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Girl_Lost:_The_Delimar_Vera_Story" title="Little Girl Lost: The Delimar Vera Story">Little Girl Lost: The Delimar Vera Story</a></i> in 2008. Ortiz also had a guest-starring role on <i><a href="/wiki/Army_Wives" title="Army Wives">Army Wives</a></i> during its second season, playing a waitress named Sandi.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She also appeared in the 2010 music video for <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias" title="Enrique Iglesias">Enrique Iglesias</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Luis_Guerra" title="Juan Luis Guerra">Juan Luis Guerra</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Cuando_Me_Enamoro" title="Cuando Me Enamoro">Cuando me enamoro</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010-present:_Devious_Maids_and_later">2010-present: <i>Devious Maids</i> and later</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010-present: Devious Maids and later">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
   <p>After <i>Ugly Betty</i> ended in 2010, Ortiz was cast as lead character in the ABC drama pilot <i>True Blue</i> about San Francisco homicide detectives reunited to solve the murder of one of their own.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The pilot was not picked up to series. The following year she appeared in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Mommas:_Like_Father,_Like_Son" title="Big Mommas: Like Father, Like Son">Big Mommas: Like Father, Like Son</a></i>, and starred in another pilot, called <i>Outnumbered</i> for Fox,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> her second pilot not picked up to series. She later had a recurring role on the HBO comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Hung_(TV_series)" title="Hung (TV series)">Hung</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Ortiz also starred in the independent films <i>Sleeping with the Fishes</i>, and <i>Such Good People</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>In 2012, Ortiz was cast as a lead character on the ABC comedy-drama pilot <i><a href="/wiki/Devious_Maids" title="Devious Maids">Devious Maids</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> While ABC chose not to pick it up to series,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Lifetime picked up the pilot with a thirteen-episode order.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The series premiered June 23, 2013, on Lifetime with positive reviews from critics.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In the show, she plays the lead role of Marisol Duarte (the alias of Professor Marisol Suarez). Also in 2013, Ortiz was special guest star in the ABC primetime soap opera, <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_(TV_series)" title="Revenge (TV series)">Revenge</a></i>, as Bizzy Preston.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In 2014, Ortiz guest-starred in episodes of two ABC shows: <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Get_Away_with_Murder" title="How to Get Away with Murder">How to Get Away with Murder</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Black-ish" title="Black-ish">Black-ish</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>In 2014 Ortiz was cast in the gay-drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Is_All_You_Need%3F_(2016_film)" title="Love Is All You Need? (2016 film)">Love is All You Need?</a></i> (2016), based on the 2011 short film with the same name.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> She did voice over work in the 2018 film <i><a href="/wiki/Ralph_Breaks_the_Internet" title="Ralph Breaks the Internet">Ralph Breaks the Internet</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> </p>

The following year, Ortiz starred in her third unsold pilot, a comedy called <i>Charlie Foxtrot</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Since 2019, Ortiz appears in the main cast of the comedy drama <i><a href="/wiki/Whiskey_Cavalier" title="Whiskey Cavalier">Whiskey Cavalier</a></i>, playing FBI <a href="/wiki/Offender_profiling" title="Offender profiling">profiler</a> Susan Sampson.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Ortiz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Ortiz married musician Noah Lebenzon in June 2007 in <a href="/wiki/Rincon,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Rincon, Puerto Rico">Rincón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Their daughter Paloma Louise Lebenzon was born June 25, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Their second child, son Rafael, was born on September 24, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Ortiz is active in raising awareness against domestic abuse, citing her own experiences when she was in her 20s and involved in a romantic relationship that turned physically abusive. In an article for <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i>, Ortiz notes that the final scene from the <i>Ugly Betty</i> episode "<a href="/wiki/How_Betty_Got_Her_Grieve_Back" title="How Betty Got Her Grieve Back">How Betty Got Her Grieve Back</a>", in which Hilda sat on her bed staring at the bathroom after realizing that Santos had died, was drawn from that experience: "I used some of that subconsciously ... It's something that is always with me".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> </p>
