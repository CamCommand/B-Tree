   <p><b>Prince Hachiko</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">蜂子皇子</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hachiko no Ōji</i>, 542–641)</span> was the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Sushun" title="Emperor Sushun">Emperor Sushun</a>, the 32nd <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">Emperor of Japan</a> who reigned from 587 to 592. His mother was <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Ctomo_no_Koteko" title="Ōtomo no Koteko">Ōtomo no Koteko</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Sushun's empress consort.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>After the assassination of his father in 592, Hachiko was forced to flee the <a href="/wiki/Soga_clan" title="Soga clan">Soga clan</a>. He made his way north along the western seacoast of Honshū. He came ashore in <a href="/wiki/Dewa_Province" title="Dewa Province">Dewa Province</a>, where he invested the rest of his life in religious pursuits.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Prince Hachiko is traditionally venerated at an imperial tomb on the top of <a href="/wiki/Mt._Haguro" class="mw-redirect" title="Mt. Haguro">Mt. Haguro</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Tsuruoka" class="mw-redirect" title="Tsuruoka">Tsuruoka</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Agency" title="Imperial Household Agency">Imperial Household Agency</a> designates this location at <a href="/wiki/Dewa_Sanzan" class="mw-redirect" title="Dewa Sanzan">Dewa Sanzan</a> as Hachiko Shrine<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">蜂子神社</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hachiko-jinja</i>)</span>. The tomb was guarded by Imperial soldiers up until the end of World War II.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
