   <p><b>Tallet 86</b> is a village in the <a href="/wiki/Gaza_Strip" title="Gaza Strip">Gaza Strip</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_territories" title="Palestinian territories">Palestinian territories</a>, around 8 kilometres (5.0&nbsp;mi) to the north-east of the town of <a href="/wiki/Khan_Yunis" title="Khan Yunis">Khan Yunis</a> and to the east of the main line connecting towns in the sector to <a href="/wiki/Rafah" title="Rafah">Rafah</a>. It is situated halfway between the towns of <a href="/wiki/Deir_al-Balah" title="Deir al-Balah">Deir al-Balah</a> and Khan Yunis. It has an area under 1,000 acres (400&nbsp;ha), and is 9&nbsp;km (5.6&nbsp;mi) from the sea, with views to the sea and the <a href="/wiki/Green_Line_(Israel)" title="Green Line (Israel)">Green Line</a>. </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponymy">Toponymy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tallet_86&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Toponymy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The name "Tallet" in <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> signifies a hill. Residents suggest that the village took its name from the number of Israeli army unit that was stationed there. (In 1948, a decisive battle was fought at Tallet which stopped the <a href="/wiki/Zionist" class="mw-redirect" title="Zionist">Zionist</a> advance in the sector, who were stopped in their tracks.) An alternative explanation is that it derives from the village being, supposedly, 86 meters above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tallet_86&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_Central_Bureau_of_Statistics" title="Palestinian Central Bureau of Statistics">Palestinian Central Bureau of Statistics</a>, in 2007 the village had a population of around 2000,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> dispersed among residential communities near sources of water and the main road. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tallet_86&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>The region's main way of life is agricultural. The village is known for growing <a href="/wiki/Citrus_fruit" class="mw-redirect" title="Citrus fruit">citrus fruits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Almond" title="Almond">almonds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olive" title="Olive">olives</a> and vegetables. The region is also noted for <a href="/wiki/Sheep_farming" title="Sheep farming">sheep farming</a>. </p>
   <p>Residents joined forces to build a primary school without a playground or surrounding walls. The village has no secondary school, therefor, students can only complete their studies at schools in Deir al-Balah. Medical provision is poor; there is one clinic. The main reason is lack of resources. Residents rely on hospitals in Gaza and Khan Yunis. Social, charitable and cultural institutions do not exist as well. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tallet_86&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>There is no transportation in the region. The village has no paved roads. Most houses have electricity, but water can only be drawn from private wells. Water is carried by animals, the residents' only mode of transport. </p>
