   <p><b>Samuel Martin Kier</b> (July 19, 1813 – October 6, 1874) was an American inventor and businessman who is credited with founding the American <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a> <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">refining</a> industry. He was the first person in the United States to refine crude oil into lamp oil.<sup id="cite_ref-Pioneer_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pioneer-1">[1]</a></sup> Kier has been dubbed the <i>Grandfather of the American Oil Industry</i> by historians.<sup id="cite_ref-McInnis_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McInnis-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Kier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

   <p>Kier was born in <a href="/wiki/Conemaugh_Township,_Indiana_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Conemaugh Township, Indiana County, Pennsylvania">Conemaugh Township</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Indiana County, Pennsylvania">Indiana County, Pennsylvania</a> near the town of <a href="/wiki/Livermore,_Pennsylvania" title="Livermore, Pennsylvania">Livermore</a>. He was the son of Thomas Kier and Mary Martin Kier. The Kiers were <a href="/wiki/Scotch-Irish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Scotch-Irish American">Scots-Irish</a> <a href="/wiki/Immigrants" class="mw-redirect" title="Immigrants">immigrants</a> who owned several <a href="/wiki/Salt_well" title="Salt well">salt wells</a> around Livermore and nearby <a href="/wiki/Saltsburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Saltsburg, Pennsylvania">Saltsburg</a>. </p>
   <p>In addition to the salt business, Samuel helped found Kier, Royer and Co., in 1838. The company was a canal boat operation that shipped coal between <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>. Kier also owned interest in several coal mines, a brickyard, and a pottery factory. </p>
   <p>He, along with several other investors including Benjamin F. Jones, founded several iron foundries in west central Pennsylvania. The iron business would be the forerunner of the <a href="/wiki/Jones_and_Laughlin_Steel_Company" title="Jones and Laughlin Steel Company">Jones and Laughlin Steel Company</a>, one of the largest steel producers in America. </p> 
   <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Kier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
   <p>Kier married Nancy Eicher of <a href="/wiki/Greensburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Greensburg, Pennsylvania">Greensburg</a>, Pennsylvania in 1842. </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Salt_wells_and_oil">Salt wells and oil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Kier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Salt wells and oil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>By the 1840s, Kier's <a href="/wiki/Salt_well" title="Salt well">salt wells</a> were becoming fouled with petroleum. At first, Kier simply dumped the useless oil into the nearby <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Main_Line_Canal" class="mw-redirect" title="Pennsylvania Main Line Canal">Pennsylvania Main Line Canal</a>, but after an oil slick caught fire, he saw a way to profit from this otherwise worthless byproduct. With no formal training in science or <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, he began experimenting with several distillates of the crude oil along with a chemist from eastern Pennsylvania. He developed a substance he named "Rock Oil" and later "Seneca Oil". In 1848, he began packaging the substance as a <a href="/wiki/Patent_medicine" title="Patent medicine">patent medicine</a> charging $0.50 per bottle.<sup id="cite_ref-Harper_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harper-3">[3]</a></sup> He also produced petroleum butter (<a href="/wiki/Petroleum_jelly" title="Petroleum jelly">petroleum jelly</a>) and sold it as a topical ointment.<sup id="cite_ref-Welty_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welty-4">[4]</a></sup> Neither product proved to be a commercial success. </p>
   <p>After further experimenting, he discovered an economical way to produce <a href="/wiki/Kerosene" title="Kerosene">kerosene</a>. Kerosene had been known for some time but was not widely produced and was considered to have little economic value. But at the time <a href="/wiki/Whale_oil" title="Whale oil">whale oil</a>, the principal fuel for lamps in America, was becoming increasingly scarce and expensive. </p>
   <p>Kier began selling the kerosene, named "Carbon Oil", to local miners in 1851. He also invented a new lamp to burn his product.<sup id="cite_ref-Pioneer_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pioneer-1">[1]</a></sup> Kier never obtained a patent for his developments and many other inventors and businessmen would go on to improve upon his work yielding huge fortunes. Even so, Kier's income at the time exceeded US$40,000 per year, a huge sum for the time. </p>
   <p>Kier established America's first oil refinery in Pittsburgh on Seventh avenue near Grant Street, in 1853.<sup id="cite_ref-Pioneer_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pioneer-1">[1]</a></sup> A marker identifying the site reads "Kier Refinery – Using a five-barrel still, Samuel M. Kier erected on this site about 1854 the first commercial refinery to produce illuminating oil from petroleum. He used crude oil from salt wells at <a href="/wiki/Tarentum,_Pennsylvania" title="Tarentum, Pennsylvania">Tarentum</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Kier consulted with <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Drake" title="Edwin Drake">Edwin Drake</a> concerning Drake's experimental <a href="/wiki/Oil_well" title="Oil well">oil well</a><sup id="cite_ref-Wright_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wright-6">[6]</a></sup> and the first shipment of oil from Drake's well went to Kier's refinery.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> </p>
