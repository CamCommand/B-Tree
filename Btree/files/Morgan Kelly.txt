   <p><b>Morgan Kelly</b> is Professor of Economics at <a href="/wiki/University_College_Dublin" title="University College Dublin">University College Dublin</a> (UCD). Described by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Irish_Times" title="The Irish Times">The Irish Times</a></i> as the country's official <a href="/wiki/Oracle" title="Oracle">soothsayer</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Kelly has notably predicted the bursting of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_property_bubble" title="Irish property bubble">Irish property bubble</a>. </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morgan_Kelly_(economist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Kelly studied at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> in the United States. He was Assistant Professor at <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a>, College Lecturer at UCD and is now Professor at UCD. He has refereed numerous international economic journals.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Predictions">Predictions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morgan_Kelly_(economist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Predictions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
    Main article: 
    <a href="/wiki/Irish_property_bubble" title="Irish property bubble">Irish property bubble</a>
   </div> 
   <p>Kelly predicted in 2006 that property prices were going to crash by 50% based on empirical evidence of past property crashes. Kelly has garnered praise from fellow economists for his prediction of the collapse of the property market.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
   <p> In July 2007, <a href="/wiki/Taoiseach" title="Taoiseach">Taoiseach</a> <a href="/wiki/Bertie_Ahern" title="Bertie Ahern">Bertie Ahern</a> implicitly criticised Kelly for his articles predicting a property crash:</p>
   <blockquote>
    <p>Sitting on the sidelines, cribbing and moaning is a lost opportunity. I don't know how people who engage in that don't commit suicide because frankly the only thing that motivates me is being able to actively change something.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
   </blockquote> 
   <p>Ahern subsequently apologised for the reference to suicide in his remark.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>Kelly takes the view that the Irish state should not have put itself forward as guarantor for the Irish banks in September 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>As of May 2011, Kelly suggests that bankruptcy for the state of Ireland is a possibility.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>
