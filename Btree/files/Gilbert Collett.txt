   <p><a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Colonel" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant Colonel">Lt. Colonel</a> <b>Gilbert Faraday Collett</b> <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">DSO</a> (18 July 1879 – 25 February 1945)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was an English sportsman who was an international <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> wing and <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class cricketer</a> for <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Gloucestershire County Cricket Club">Gloucestershire County Cricket Club</a>. As a rugby footballer he represented <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University_R.U.F.C." title="Cambridge University R.U.F.C.">Cambridge University</a> and Cheltenham at club level. Collett also played international rugby for the <a href="/wiki/British_and_Irish_Lions" title="British and Irish Lions">British Isles</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1903_British_Lions_tour_to_South_Africa" title="1903 British Lions tour to South Africa">1903 tour of South Africa</a> but was never selected for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England national team</a>. </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_history">Personal history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Collett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Collett was born in 1879 in Wynstone Place, near Gloucester to John Martin Collett, of Guy's Cliff, Wolton. He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Cheltenham_College" title="Cheltenham College">Cheltenham College</a> before matriculating to <a href="/wiki/Pembroke_College,_Cambridge" title="Pembroke College, Cambridge">Pembroke College, Cambridge</a> in 1898.<sup id="cite_ref-Venn_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Venn-2">[2]</a></sup> He gained his BA in 1901, and his MA in 1905, before becoming managing director of family company, J. M. Collett and Co. Ltd, a chemical manufacturer. </p>
   <p>With the outbreak of the First World War, Collett joined the British Army and was posted to the 5th Battalion of the <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire_Regiment" title="Gloucestershire Regiment">Gloucestershire Regiment</a>. On 12 August 1914 he was promoted to captain.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> During the war Collett was <a href="/wiki/Mentioned_in_Despatches" class="mw-redirect" title="Mentioned in Despatches">Mentioned in Despatches</a> three times and was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">Distinguished Service Order</a>. In 1934, on reaching the age of 55 he was retired from the forces, but retained his rank, Lieutenant Colonel, and uniform.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_career">Rugby career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Collett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rugby career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Collett first came to note as a rugby player when he represented Cambridge University. In 1898, Collett was part of the Cambridge team that faced <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_RFC" title="Oxford University RFC">Oxford University</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Varsity_Match" title="The Varsity Match">the Varsity Match</a>, winning his first and only sporting 'Blue'. After Oxford lost Nelson, their captain, to an ankle injury, Cambridge took control of the forward positions and won the game comfortably. The 1898/99 season saw Collett invited to join touring team, <a href="/wiki/Barbarian_F.C." title="Barbarian F.C.">the Barbarians</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
   <p>By 1903, Collett was playing club rugby for Cheltenham and county rugby for Gloucestershire. Although he had not played international rugby before, he was offered a place on the 1903 British Isles tour of South Africa. Collett played in 20 of the 22 match tour, including all three Test games against the <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_rugby_union_team" title="South Africa national rugby union team">South African national team</a>. Collett was a prolific scoring during the first half of the tour, with a dropped goal in his first match against an invitational Western Province Country team, followed by eight <a href="/wiki/Try" title="Try">tries</a> over the next 11 games, including two tries in both games against King William's Town and <a href="/wiki/Griquas_(rugby)" title="Griquas (rugby)">Griqualand West</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In the final eight games he was selected for, including the three tests, Collett failed to add to his score tally. </p>
   <p>During the First World War, Collett was touch judge for a rugby match played between British troops from the Gloucestershire Regiment, which included club county and international players. The match was played between the South Midland Division (48th) and the 4th Division.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p>
