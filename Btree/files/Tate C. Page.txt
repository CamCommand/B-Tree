   <p><b>Tate Cromwell "Piney" Page</b> (January 6, 1908 – May 19, 1984) was an <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> player and coach. He served as the head football coach at <a href="/wiki/Transylvania_University" title="Transylvania University">Transylvania University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lexington,_Kentucky" title="Lexington, Kentucky">Lexington, Kentucky</a> from 1938 to 1941 and at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Central_Missouri" title="University of Central Missouri">University of Central Missouri</a> from 1948 to 1951.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Page was the head coach for Central Missouri State during the famous <a href="/wiki/1951_Central_Missouri_State_vs._Southwestern_football_game" title="1951 Central Missouri State vs. Southwestern football game">1951 Central Missouri State vs. Southwestern football game</a> in which a team rejected a touchdown awarded by the game officials. He was later dean of the College of Education at <a href="/wiki/Western_Kentucky_University" title="Western Kentucky University">Western Kentucky University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Page graduated from <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_Tech_University" title="Arkansas Tech University">Arkansas Tech University</a> in 1930 and was a football letter-winner at <a href="/wiki/Tulane_University" title="Tulane University">Tulane Universityin</a> 1933.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p>
