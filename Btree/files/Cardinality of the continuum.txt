   <p>This was proven by <a href="/wiki/Georg_Cantor" title="Georg Cantor">Georg Cantor</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Cantor%27s_first_uncountability_proof" class="mw-redirect" title="Cantor's first uncountability proof">1874 uncountability proof</a>, part of his groundbreaking study of different infinities; the inequality was later stated more simply in his <a href="/wiki/Cantor%27s_diagonal_argument" title="Cantor's diagonal argument">diagonal argument</a>. Cantor defined cardinality in terms of <a href="/wiki/Bijective_function" class="mw-redirect" title="Bijective function">bijective functions</a>: two sets have the same cardinality if and only if there exists a bijective function between them. </p>
   <p>Between any two real numbers <i>a</i>&nbsp;&lt;&nbsp;<i>b</i>, no matter how close they are to each other, there are always infinitely many other real numbers, and Cantor showed that they are as many as those contained in the whole set of real numbers.
