   <p><b>Joe Allison</b> is a former <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> <a href="/wiki/Placekicker" title="Placekicker">placekicker</a>. Allison is perhaps most famous for being the winner of the <a href="/wiki/Inaugural" class="mw-redirect" title="Inaugural">inaugural</a> <a href="/wiki/Lou_Groza_Award" title="Lou Groza Award">Lou Groza Award</a> in 1992, the award presented to honor the top placekicker in <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SI_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SI-1">[1]</a></sup> He went on to play in one game for the <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Mad_Dogs" title="Memphis Mad Dogs">Memphis Mad Dogs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Football_League" title="Canadian Football League">Canadian Football League</a> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>

   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Allison_(gridiron_football)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="High_school">High school</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Allison_(gridiron_football)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: High school">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
   <p>Allison attended high school at <a href="/wiki/American_High_School_(Miami-Dade_County,_Florida)" title="American High School (Miami-Dade County, Florida)">American High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a>. Although he starred at his school as a <a href="/wiki/Quarterback" title="Quarterback">quarterback</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punter_(American_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Punter (American football)">punter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Placekicker" title="Placekicker">placekicker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Memphis_State_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Memphis State University">Memphis State University</a> was the only <a href="/wiki/Division_1-A" class="mw-redirect" title="Division 1-A">Division 1-A</a> school to offer him a <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SI_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SI-1">[1]</a></sup> Allison chose to accept the scholarship and attend Memphis State. </p> 
   <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="College">College</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Allison_(gridiron_football)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: College">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
   <p>Allison played collegiate football at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Memphis" title="University of Memphis">University of Memphis</a>, which at the time was called <a href="/wiki/Memphis_State_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Memphis State University">Memphis State University</a>. In 1992, his junior season at the school, Allison won the <a href="/wiki/Lou_Groza_Award" title="Lou Groza Award">Lou Groza Award</a>, awarded to the nation's top <a href="/wiki/Placekicker" title="Placekicker">placekicker</a>. During the season, Allison made 23 out of 25 field goals, and led the <a href="/wiki/NCAA" class="mw-redirect" title="NCAA">NCAA</a> in both field goals made as well as field goal percentage, where he achieved 92 percent. He also connected on all 32 extra points that he attempted. For the 1992 season, Allison scored a total of 101 points. Allison was also named by the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> to the first-team <a href="/wiki/1992_College_Football_All-America_Team" title="1992 College Football All-America Team">All-American</a> team, the first Memphis State University player in the school's history to be honored with that recognition.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional">Professional</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Allison_(gridiron_football)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Professional">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
   <p>Allison played a single regular season game for the Memphis Mad Dogs of the Canadian Football League, in which he completed six kick-offs, scored one field goal on three attempts, and made two <a href="/wiki/Single_(football)" title="Single (football)">singles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> </p> 
   <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Allison_(gridiron_football)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
   <p>Joe Allison's <a href="/wiki/Cousin" title="Cousin">first cousin</a> was <a href="/wiki/Davey_Allison" title="Davey Allison">Davey Allison</a>, who was a <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> <a href="/wiki/Race_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Race car">race car</a> driver for <a href="/wiki/Robert_Yates_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Yates Racing">Robert Yates Racing</a>. Davey was killed in a <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> accident in 1993. Joe was the <a href="/wiki/Godparent" title="Godparent">godfather</a> to Davey's daughter Krista.<sup id="cite_ref-SI_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SI-1">[1]</a></sup> Joe and Davey were very close, and after his death, Joe said "Before I go in to kick, I put my hat down and say, 'Davey, give me the strength to do the best I can, to be as strong as you were.'"<sup id="cite_ref-SI_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SI-1">[1]</a></sup> Joe is currently a special education teacher at North Forsyth High School in Cumming, Georgia. </p>
