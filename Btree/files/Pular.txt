   <p><b>Pular</b> is a massive <a href="/wiki/Stratovolcano" title="Stratovolcano">stratovolcano</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Antofagasta_Region" title="Antofagasta Region">Antofagasta Region</a> of northern <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, about 15&nbsp;km west of the border with <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, which in this area is a straight line between the summits of <a href="/wiki/Socompa" title="Socompa">Socompa</a> volcano and <i>Cerro del Rincón</i>. Pular, along with <i>Cerro Pajonales</i>, forms a high volcanic ridge, which runs in a generally north-east to south-west direction for 12&nbsp;km (7&nbsp;mi). Southward, following the same direction as the ridge, lies Socompa volcano. The ridge's crest forms a <a href="/wiki/Drainage_divide" title="Drainage divide">drainage divide</a> between <a href="/wiki/Salar_de_Atacama" title="Salar de Atacama">Salar de Atacama</a> basin and <i>Salar de Pular</i> basin. This latter is a bowl-shaped basin enclosed on the east by <a href="/wiki/Aracar" title="Aracar">Aracar</a> volcano. </p>
   <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Kunza_language" title="Kunza language">Kunza</a> language Pular means "The Eyebrow".<sup id="cite_ref-aaj_1974_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aaj_1974-3">[3]</a></sup> </p>
